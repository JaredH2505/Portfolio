Ecosim

An economy simulation engine

EcoSim is an economy engine for Unity game development. As games have increased in complexity, the development time required to complete them has increased significantly and requires that companies developing games decide which features to include in their games. Unfortunately, in-game economies are often one of the features that goes by the wayside. Realistic economies are pared down to simple models where trading exists only between the player and NPCs, prices are fixed, and supply is regenerated at fixed time intervals. This decision helps games stay on schedule, but it also detracts from the immersiveness of those games. EcoSim aims to change this.

Just as the introduction of physics engines in the early 2000s allowed game developers to incorporate high-quality physics simulations into their games, EcoSim aims to make it fast and easy for game developers to incorporate realistic economies into their games as well.

EcoSim will be distributed as a Unity package that can be incorporated directly and easily into any Unity game. Once the package is installed, developers will be able to apply EcoSim scripts to game objects and configure the EcoSim engine to dial in the specifics of the economy they want to simulate in their game. This configuration will allow developers to specify everything from the prices of goods, cost of travel, and production rates to the roles and needs of the economic agents (NPCs) in their game.

Once configured, EcoSim will handle the specifics of running the in-game economy by managing the supply of goods, their prices, and the actions of economic agents that are producing, consuming, transporting, buying, and selling goods. Because EcoSim handles the specifics, developers using the EcoSim package will be able to include a realistic economy in their games without having to spend the time to develop the economic engine themselves, which will make their games more immersive and fun to play. ~ Team EcoSim
